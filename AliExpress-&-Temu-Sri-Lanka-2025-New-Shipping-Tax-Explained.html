What Changed in 2025?

Sri Lanka has tightened import rules for eCommerce. Customs now reviews all international parcels regardless of declared value. This means AliExpress and Temu orders are no longer "tax-free" under LKR 3,000.

How Are Taxes Applied?

Customs calculates taxes based on estimated market value, not declared value. Duties, VAT (usually 15%), and handling fees are added. Even if your item was cheap, the shipping value or bulk order can trigger taxes.

Common Scenarios

  • Under-declared item? Customs may assign a higher value and charge accordingly.
  • Free shipping? Still taxable based on the item's assessed value.
  • High-volume buyers? Risk further scrutiny or business classification.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I have to pay tax for AliExpress parcels in Sri Lanka?

Yes. Customs requires tax payments for most online orders entering Sri Lanka, including those from Temu and AliExpress.

How is the shipping tax calculated?

The final tax is based on Customs' market value assessment. VAT (15%), import duty, and sometimes port handling fees are applied.
