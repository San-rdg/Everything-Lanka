Welcome to Anuradhapura

District

Anuradhapura is one of the most historically and culturally significant districts in Sri Lanka. As the first ancient capital of the country, it stands as a symbol of the island’s rich heritage and civilization.

History & Heritage

Anuradhapura served as the capital of Sri Lanka for over a millennium. It is a UNESCO World Heritage Site, renowned for its ancient architecture, stupas, and sacred places of worship. The city is deeply rooted in Buddhist history and was once a flourishing spiritual and cultural hub.

Religious Significance

The district is home to some of the most sacred Buddhist landmarks in the world, such as Jaya Sri Maha Bodhi—a sapling of the original Bodhi tree under which Buddha attained enlightenment, and the massive Ruwanwelisaya Stupa, revered by Buddhists across the globe.

Agriculture & Irrigation

Anuradhapura is also an agricultural stronghold. The ancient kings of Sri Lanka built complex irrigation systems with large tanks like Nuwara Wewa, Tissa Wewa, and Basawakkulama to support farming. Paddy cultivation remains the district's backbone.

Tourist Attractions

  • Ruwanwelisaya: One of the most sacred and iconic stupas in Sri Lanka, built by King Dutugemunu.
  • Jaya Sri Maha Bodhi: The oldest recorded living tree in the world with a known planting date.
  • Abhayagiri & Jetavanaramaya: Gigantic stupas and ancient monastic complexes.
  • Isurumuniya Temple: Famous for the "Isurumuniya Lovers" rock carving.
  • Mihintale: A mountain peak believed to be where Buddhism was first introduced to Sri Lanka.

Culture & Lifestyle

People of Anuradhapura live simple, respectful lives. The culture is deeply influenced by Buddhism, and festivals like Poson Poya attract thousands of pilgrims annually. Traditional village life thrives alongside modern developments in the town area.

Connectivity

Anuradhapura is well-connected by road and rail. It is a key transit city in the North Central Province and easily accessible from Colombo and Kandy. Public buses and trains operate regularly, and the city also hosts an airport for domestic flights.

Interesting Facts

  • Anuradhapura was the political and religious capital of Sri Lanka from the 4th century BC to the 11th century AD.
  • The irrigation systems built here are still functional and admired globally.
  • The city is one of the oldest continuously inhabited places in the world.
  • It is considered the cradle of Sinhalese Buddhist civilization.

Visiting Anuradhapura is like stepping back in time. With its sacred atmosphere, ancient wonders, and timeless stories, it offers a truly spiritual and enlightening experience to every traveler.
