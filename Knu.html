Knuckles Mountain Range, Sri Lanka

Knuckles Mountain Range, a UNESCO World Heritage Site, is a breathtaking destination in Sri Lanka, known for its striking landscapes, biodiversity, and stunning views of the surrounding valleys. It's an ideal spot for trekking, nature walks, and photography.

Top Attractions

  • **Trekking trails** offering panoramic views of mist-covered mountains and valleys.
  • **Riverston Peak**, known for its sweeping vistas of the entire range.
  • Explore the **Horton Plains National Park** located nearby, with its famous World’s End cliff.
  • Visit **meandering streams** and waterfalls hidden deep within the forest.

How to Get There

  • Located about **25 km from Kandy**, it can be reached via the A26 highway.
  • From Kandy, take a **bus or private vehicle** to the town of Matale, then head to the Knuckles Range.
  • The journey to Knuckles involves some **rugged terrain**, making it perfect for off-road enthusiasts.

Best Time to Visit

The best time to visit is between **December and April** when the weather is cool and dry, making it ideal for trekking and outdoor activities.

Interesting Facts

  • The Knuckles range gets its name from the shape of its peaks that resemble a clenched fist.
  • Home to **unique flora and fauna**, including rare species like the **Sri Lankan Leopard** and several types of orchids.
  • The area is popular with **birdwatchers**, with over 100 species of birds recorded here.
  • Rich in **cultural history**, with several ancient temples and villages scattered across the range.

Knuckles offers a unique blend of adventure, natural beauty, and cultural richness. If you're looking for a serene yet adventurous getaway, this is the place to be!
