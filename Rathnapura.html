Welcome to Ratnapura

Ratnapura District

Ratnapura is known as the **"City of Gems"** in Sri Lanka. It is famous for its **gem mining industry**, lush **rainforests**, and breathtaking **waterfalls**. The district is rich in natural beauty and plays a vital role in the country’s **gem trade**.

The Gem Capital of Sri Lanka

Ratnapura has been the heart of Sri Lanka’s **gemstone industry** for centuries. Precious stones such as **sapphires, rubies, and cat’s eyes** are mined here and exported worldwide.

Natural Attractions

The district is home to some of **Sri Lanka’s most scenic waterfalls and mountains**. The surrounding forests and tea estates make it a paradise for nature lovers.

Tourist Attractions

  • Adam’s Peak (Sri Pada): A sacred pilgrimage site known for its footprint-shaped rock at the summit.
  • Bopath Ella Waterfall: A spectacular waterfall shaped like a **Bo leaf**.
  • Sinharaja Rainforest: A UNESCO World Heritage Site and one of the last remaining **primary rainforests** in Sri Lanka.
  • Gemmological Museum: Showcases the history and craftsmanship of the Sri Lankan **gem industry**.
  • Katugas Ella Waterfall: A hidden natural gem perfect for trekking and photography.

Culture & Lifestyle

Ratnapura has a unique culture influenced by **Buddhism, Hinduism, and the gem trade**. The district hosts many **traditional festivals**, including pilgrimages to **Sri Pada (Adam’s Peak)**.

Connectivity

Ratnapura is well-connected by **road and rail**, making it accessible from **Colombo, Kandy, and Galle**. The district’s highways pass through scenic **tea estates and forests**, offering stunning views.

Interesting Facts

  • Ratnapura means **"City of Gems"** in Sinhala.
  • It is the main gem-mining region in Sri Lanka, known for producing **blue sapphires**.
  • Adam’s Peak is one of the most sacred mountains in **Buddhism, Hinduism, and Islam**.
  • Sinharaja Rainforest is home to **endemic species** found nowhere else in the world.

With its **gem mines, mountains, waterfalls, and rainforests**, Ratnapura is a **hidden treasure** waiting to be explored. Whether you're a **nature lover, adventurer, or gemstone enthusiast**, this district has something special for everyone.
