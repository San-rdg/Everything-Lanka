The Sri Lanka Tourism Development Authority (SLTDA) is the official government body responsible for regulating and promoting the tourism industry in Sri Lanka. Under the Tourism Act No. 38 of 2005, all tourism-related businesses ‚Äî including hotels, guesthouses, homestays, tour operators, and service providers ‚Äî must obtain official SLTDA registration to operate legally. This registration ensures compliance with national standards for safety, quality, and guest protection, while providing businesses with legal recognition, credibility, and access to government support programs.

  • Hotels ‚Äî All star-rated and non-star hotels, from budget to luxury
  • Guest Houses ‚Äî Small-scale accommodation with 1-15 rooms
  • Homestays ‚Äî Family homes offering rooms to tourists
  • Villas ‚Äî Private vacation rental properties

üìù Step-by-Step Registration Process

1

Create an SLTDA Account

Visit the official SLTDA online registration portal at www.sltda.gov.lk/registration. Create a new business account by providing:

  • Business owner's full name (as per NIC/passport)
  • Business name and registration number
  • Contact details (phone, email, physical address)
  • Business category selection
  • Valid email address for verification

You'll receive a confirmation email with login credentials. Keep these secure for future access.

2

Choose Your Business Category

Select the appropriate category from the SLTDA classification system:

  • Accommodation: Hotels, Guesthouses, Homestays, Villas, Boutique Hotels, Hostels
  • Tour Services: Tour Operators, Travel Agents, Inbound Operators
  • Guides: National Tourist Guide, Chauffeur Guide, Site Guide
  • Activity Providers: Water sports, Adventure tourism, Safari operators
  • Wellness: Spa, Ayurveda centers, Health tourism facilities
  • Transport: Vehicle rental, boat services, tourist transport

Important: Your category determines applicable fees, standards, and inspection requirements.

3

Upload Required Documents

Prepare and upload digital copies of all mandatory documents:

  • NIC or Passport: Clear scanned copy of owner's identification
  • Business Registration Certificate: From Registrar of Companies or local authority
  • Location Images: High-quality photos of exterior, entrance, rooms, facilities (minimum 10 photos)
  • Floor Plan: Architectural drawings showing room layouts, dimensions, exits
  • Fire Safety Certificate: From Fire Department (for buildings with 6+ rooms)
  • Public Liability Insurance: Valid insurance policy covering guest injuries
  • Waste Management Plan: Documentation of waste disposal procedures
  • Land Ownership/Lease Agreement: Proof of property rights
  • Tax Clearance Certificate: From Inland Revenue Department

File Format: PDF or JPEG, maximum 5MB per file. Ensure all documents are clear and legible.

4

Pay Registration Fees

Complete payment through approved methods:

  • Online Payment: Credit/debit card through secure SLTDA portal
  • Bank Transfer: Direct deposit to SLTDA bank account (provide reference number)
  • In-Person Payment: At SLTDA head office or district offices

Registration fees vary by business type and size (see Fees section below). Payment confirmation is required before inspection scheduling.

5

SLTDA Inspection

An authorized SLTDA inspector will visit your premises to verify compliance:

  • Inspection Scheduling: You'll receive 7-14 days advance notice with date and time
  • Compliance Checklist: Inspectors evaluate safety, cleanliness, room standards, facilities
  • Safety Requirements: Fire extinguishers, emergency exits, first aid, lighting
  • Quality Standards: Room size, ventilation, bedding quality, bathroom facilities
  • Documentation Review: Verification of submitted documents against physical property

Inspection Duration: Typically 2-4 hours depending on property size. Ensure all facilities are accessible and clean.

6

Receive Your License Certificate

Upon successful inspection and approval:

  • You'll receive official SLTDA registration certificate via email (digital copy)
  • Physical certificate mailed to registered business address within 10 working days
  • Certificate includes: Registration number, business name, category, validity period
  • Validity Period: Most registrations valid for 1 year from issue date
  • Display Requirement: Certificate must be prominently displayed at business premises
  • Renewal: Apply for renewal 30 days before expiry to avoid lapses

üí° Application Timeline

Total Duration: 2-6 weeks from submission to certificate issuance

  • Document verification: 3-5 business days
  • Inspection scheduling: 1-3 weeks (varies by district and season)
  • On-site inspection: 1 day
  • Final approval and certificate issuance: 3-7 business days

Peak Season Delays: Applications submitted October-December may take longer due to high volume.

‚úÖ SLTDA Standards & Compliance Requirements

Accommodation Quality Standards

Minimum Room Requirements

  • Room Size: Minimum 10 sq meters (108 sq feet) for single occupancy, 12 sq meters (130 sq feet) for double
  • Bed Standards: Quality mattresses minimum 6 inches thick, clean linens, pillows, blankets
  • Ventilation: Windows with screens or air conditioning, adequate natural light
  • Bathroom: Private attached bathroom with Western or Asian toilet, shower/bath, hot water provision
  • Furniture: Wardrobe/hanging space, bedside table, chair/seating, mirror
  • Lighting: Reading lights, main lighting minimum 100 lux, emergency lighting
  • Privacy: Locks on room doors, curtains/blinds on windows

Safety & Fire Systems

  • Fire Extinguishers: ABC type extinguishers on every floor, serviced annually
  • Emergency Exits: Clearly marked, unobstructed, minimum 2 exits for buildings 2+ floors
  • Smoke Detectors: In hallways and common areas (mandatory for 10+ rooms)
  • Emergency Lighting: Battery backup lighting along exit routes
  • Fire Assembly Point: Designated safe outdoor gathering area
  • First Aid Kit: Stocked kit accessible to staff, with trained first aid personnel
  • Emergency Contact List: Posted in reception and rooms (police, fire, hospital)

Cleanliness Standards

  • Daily Housekeeping: Room cleaning, bed making, towel changes
  • Linen Standards: Clean sheets and towels for each guest, laundry facilities or service
  • Bathroom Hygiene: Daily cleaning, disinfection, quality toiletries provided
  • Pest Control: Regular professional pest management, no evidence of rodents/insects
  • Kitchen/Dining: Food hygiene certification, proper food storage, clean cooking areas
  • Swimming Pool: Daily water testing, chlorination, cleaning (if applicable)

Guest Privacy & Security

  • CCTV in common areas (not in private rooms/bathrooms)
  • Guest data protection compliance (personal information security)
  • 24-hour reception or emergency contact availability
  • Secure storage for guest valuables (safe or locked area)
  • Guest register maintenance (check-in/out records)

Environmental Guidelines

  • Waste separation (organic, recyclable, general waste)
  • Energy conservation measures (LED lighting, solar hot water encouraged)
  • Water conservation (low-flow fixtures, wastewater management)
  • No single-use plastics policy (encouraged)
  • Proper sewage treatment or connection to municipal system

üîÑ Registration Renewal Process

SLTDA registration is typically valid for 1 year from the issue date and must be renewed annually to maintain legal status.

Renewal Steps

  1. Early Application (30 Days Before Expiry)

    Log into your SLTDA portal account 30 days before your certificate expires. Check your renewal eligibility and any outstanding compliance issues.

  2. Update Information

    Review and update your business information:

    • Contact details (phone, email, address)
    • Owner/director changes (if any)
    • Room inventory updates (additions, renovations)
    • Service offerings changes
  3. Upload Updated Documents

    Provide renewed copies of expiring documents:

    • Fire safety certificate (if expired)
    • Public liability insurance policy (must be current)
    • Tax clearance certificate (current year)
    • Health certificate (if expired)
    • New property photos (if major renovations done)
  4. Pay Renewal Fees

    Renewal fees are typically 60-70% of initial registration fees. Payment methods same as initial registration (online, bank transfer, in-person).

  5. Reinspection (If Required)

    Reinspection is only required if:

    • Major renovations or structural changes made
    • Category upgrade requested (e.g., guesthouse to hotel)
    • Previous compliance issues reported
    • Random audit selection by SLTDA

    Most routine renewals without changes do not require reinspection.

  6. Receive Renewed Certificate

    Upon approval, you'll receive your renewed certificate via email within 5-7 business days. Physical certificate mailed to registered address.

‚ö†Ô∏è Late Renewal Penalties

If you fail to renew before expiry:

  • Grace Period: 30 days after expiry with 10% late fee
  • 31-60 Days Late: 20% penalty on renewal fee
  • 60+ Days Late: Registration lapses completely ‚Äî must reapply as new registration with full fees
  • Operating with Expired Registration: Illegal and subject to fines/closure

Best Practice: Set reminders 60 days and 30 days before expiry to ensure timely renewal.

‚ö†Ô∏è Common Issues & How to Fix Them

Application Rejected Due to Missing Images

Solution: Ensure you upload minimum 10 clear photos showing: exterior facade, entrance, 3-4 different guest rooms, bathrooms, dining area, kitchen (if applicable), emergency exits, and common areas. Photos must be well-lit and show room dimensions clearly.

Fire Certificate Expired

Solution: Contact Sri Lanka Fire Services to schedule renewal inspection. Book appointment 4-6 weeks in advance. Fire officers will check extinguishers, exits, and emergency procedures. Certificate renewal usually takes 1-2 weeks after passing inspection.

Incorrect Floor Area Measurements

Solution: Hire a qualified surveyor to prepare accurate floor plans with dimensions. Plans must show: total floor area, individual room sizes, bathroom dimensions, corridors, and exits. Measurements must match actual property dimensions within 5% tolerance.

Business Name Mismatch

Solution: Ensure your business name on SLTDA application exactly matches your business registration certificate. If you operate under a trading name different from registered name, provide both names and supporting documentation. Update ROC registration if needed.

Insufficient Insurance Coverage

Solution: Public liability insurance must cover minimum LKR 5 million. Contact insurance companies specializing in hospitality (Ceylinco, Sri Lanka Insurance, LOLC). Policy must specifically cover guest injuries, property damage, and third-party liability.

Failed Safety Inspection

Solution: Common failures include: blocked emergency exits, missing fire extinguishers, inadequate lighting. Request detailed inspection report, rectify all issues, and schedule re-inspection. Re-inspection fee applies (LKR 5,000-10,000).

‚è±Ô∏è How Long Does Tourist Board Registration Take?

The complete registration process from application submission to certificate receipt typically takes 2 to 6 weeks. However, duration varies based on several factors:

Registration Timeline Breakdown

Week 1: Document submission and verification (3-5 business days). SLTDA reviews all uploaded documents for completeness and accuracy.

Week 2-3: Inspection scheduling and coordination (1-3 weeks). Inspector assigned based on location. You'll receive 7-14 days advance notice with confirmed date.

Week 3-4: On-site inspection (1 day). Inspector visits property, evaluates compliance, prepares report.

Week 4-5: Inspection report review and final approval (3-7 business days). Management reviews inspection findings and makes final decision.

Week 5-6: Certificate issuance and delivery (3-5 business days). Digital certificate emailed, physical certificate mailed to address.

Factors Affecting Timeline

Peak Season Delays (October - March)

Tourist high season coincides with increased registration applications. Inspection scheduling may take 4-6 weeks during this period. Best Time to Apply: April - September for faster processing.

District Location

  • Colombo District: Fastest processing (2-3 weeks) due to nearby head office
  • Galle, Kandy, Matara: Normal processing (3-4 weeks)
  • Remote Districts (Jaffna, Trincomalee, Batticaloa): Longer processing (4-6 weeks) due to inspector travel logistics

Application Completeness

Complete applications with all documents submitted correctly process fastest. Incomplete applications requiring additional documents can add 1-2 weeks to timeline.

üöÄ Expedited Processing

SLTDA occasionally offers expedited processing for an additional 50% fee. This can reduce timeline to 1-2 weeks. Availability varies ‚Äî contact SLTDA directly to inquire. Best for urgent situations (new business opening, partnership deadlines).

‚ùì Frequently Asked Questions

How do I register with the Sri Lanka Tourist Board?

To register with SLTDA: (1) Create an account on the SLTDA online portal at www.sltda.gov.lk/registration, (2) Select your business category, (3) Upload required documents (business registration, NIC, location images, floor plans, fire certificate, insurance), (4) Pay registration fees online or at SLTDA offices, (5) Complete SLTDA on-site inspection within 2-3 weeks, (6) Receive your license certificate via email and mail. The entire process takes 2-6 weeks depending on inspection schedules and document completeness.

Is Tourist Board Registration mandatory in Sri Lanka?

Yes, SLTDA registration is legally mandatory under the Tourism Act No. 38 of 2005 for all tourism-related businesses including hotels, guesthouses, homestays, villas, tour operators, travel agencies, tourist guides, and tourism service providers. Operating without registration is a criminal offense punishable by fines up to LKR 500,000, business closure, and potential legal action. All accommodation providers accepting paying guests and all tourism service businesses must be registered.

How much does SLTDA registration cost?

SLTDA registration fees vary by business type and size: Guesthouses 1-5 rooms: LKR 5,000-15,000, Small hotels 6-20 rooms: LKR 25,000-75,000, Large hotels 20+ rooms: LKR 100,000-500,000, Homestays 1-3 rooms: LKR 5,000-10,000, Tour operators: LKR 50,000-200,000, Tourist guides: LKR 10,000-18,000. Annual renewal fees are typically 60-70% of initial registration. Additional costs include fire certificate (LKR 10,000-20,000), public liability insurance (LKR 15,000-50,000), and surveyor fees for floor plans (LKR 5,000-15,000).

Do homestays need to register with SLTDA?

Yes, homestays must register with SLTDA if offering accommodation to tourists for payment, even if operating from your family home. Homestay registration requirements include: valid business registration from local Municipal Council or Pradeshiya Sabha, owner's NIC copy, location photos (minimum 6-8 photos), basic floor plan showing guest rooms, fire safety equipment (extinguisher minimum), and proof of water supply and waste disposal. Homestay registration is more affordable (LKR 5,000-10,000) than hotel registration and has relaxed standards, but registration is still mandatory for legal operation.

Can I run a guest house without Tourist Board approval?

No, operating a guesthouse without SLTDA registration is illegal and punishable under Sri Lankan tourism law. Penalties for operating without registration include: fines up to LKR 500,000 per violation, immediate business closure order, confiscation of income earned, inability to list on online travel agencies (Booking.com, Agoda), no legal protection against guest disputes, and potential criminal charges for repeat offenses. Additionally, unregistered properties cannot obtain public liability insurance, leaving owners personally liable for guest accidents or injuries. Always register before accepting your first paying guest.

What documents are needed for SLTDA registration?

Required documents vary by business type. Core documents (all businesses): Business registration certificate, Owner's NIC or passport copy, Tax clearance certificate. Accommodation providers: Building plan approval, Floor plans with measurements, Location photos (minimum 10), Fire safety certificate (6+ rooms), Public liability insurance, Waste management plan, Land ownership deed or lease agreement, Water quality report. Tour operators: Company incorporation certificate, Vehicle fleet details and insurance, Driver/guide qualifications, Office lease agreement, Professional indemnity insurance. Tourist guides: Police clearance report, Medical fitness certificate, SLTDA guide training certificate, Language proficiency test results, Driver's license (for chauffeur guides). All documents must be current and valid ‚Äî expired certificates will result in application rejection.

How do I renew my SLTDA registration?

To renew SLTDA registration: (1) Log into your SLTDA portal account 30 days before expiry date, (2) Update any changed information (contact details, room inventory, services), (3) Upload renewed versions of expiring documents (fire certificate, insurance, tax clearance), (4) Pay renewal fees online or at SLTDA office (typically 60-70% of initial registration fee), (5) Undergo reinspection only if required (major renovations, compliance issues, random audit), (6) Receive renewed certificate via email within 5-7 business days, physical certificate mailed to address. Most routine renewals without property changes do not require reinspection. Late renewal penalties: 10% for 1-30 days late, 20% for 31-60 days late, complete reapplication required if 60+ days late.

How long does SLTDA registration take?

SLTDA registration typically takes 2-6 weeks from application submission to certificate receipt. Timeline breakdown: Document verification (3-5 days), Inspection scheduling (1-3 weeks), On-site inspection (1 day), Final approval (3-7 days), Certificate issuance (3-5 days). Delays occur during peak tourist seasons (November-March) when application volume is high. Remote districts (Jaffna, Trincomalee, Batticaloa) may take longer due to inspector travel logistics. Fastest processing: Apply during off-season (April-September), submit complete documentation first time, be flexible with inspection dates. Colombo district typically fastest (2-3 weeks). Expedited processing occasionally available for 50% additional fee, reducing timeline to 1-2 weeks.

Can foreign nationals register tourism businesses in Sri Lanka?

Yes, foreign nationals can register tourism businesses in Sri Lanka through proper business incorporation. Requirements: Valid business registration with foreign investment approval from Board of Investment (BOI) or Registrar of Companies, BOI clearance for larger operations (foreign investment exceeding USD 100,000), Local partner or director required for certain business types (tour operators, travel agencies), All standard SLTDA registration documents (business certificate, floor plans, insurance, etc.), Valid business visa and residential permits for foreign directors, Work permits for foreign staff, Proof of capital investment (bank statements, transfer receipts). Process: First incorporate company in Sri Lanka with proper foreign investment documentation, then proceed with SLTDA registration using company documents. Foreign-owned tourism businesses subject to same SLTDA standards and requirements as local businesses. Consult with business lawyer specializing in foreign investment for proper setup.
