Nine Arch Bridge Ella: A Must-See Landmark in Sri Lanka

Image credit: [Pexels]

Nine Arches Bridge

The **Nine Arches Bridge**, also known as the **Bridge in the Sky**, is one of Sri Lanka's most iconic landmarks. Located in **Ella**, this spectacular bridge is renowned for its **colonial-era architecture, stunning location, and breathtaking train crossings**.

History & Architecture

Constructed in the **British colonial era** during the early **20th century**, the bridge is a masterpiece of **stone and brick engineering**, built without a single piece of steel. It was designed by **Ceylonese engineers** and stands as a testament to the ingenuity of Sri Lankan craftsmanship.

Scenic Beauty

Surrounded by **lush green tea plantations and misty hills**, the bridge offers **breathtaking views**, especially during sunrise and sunset. Visitors often wait to witness the famous **blue train** crossing the bridge, making for a picture-perfect moment.

How to Get There

  • Located **between Ella and Demodara railway stations**.
  • A **30-minute walk** from **Ella town** through scenic trails.
  • Accessible via **tuk-tuk rides** from Ella.

Best Time to Visit

The best times to visit the bridge are **early morning** or **late afternoon** for stunning photography. If you want to see a train crossing, check the local **train schedule** to time your visit.

Interesting Facts

  • The bridge is **24 meters high** and **91 meters long**, with **nine elegant arches**.
  • It was **commissioned under British rule** but constructed entirely by **local builders** using **stone, cement, and bricks**.
  • The surrounding area is **rich in tea plantations**, adding to its picturesque charm.
  • It is often compared to famous bridges like the **Glenfinnan Viaduct** in Scotland.

Visiting the **Nine Arches Bridge** is a must for anyone traveling to **Ella**. Whether you're a **photography enthusiast, history buff, or nature lover**, this architectural wonder will leave you spellbound.
