Kalutara District

Kalutara is a picturesque coastal district in Sri Lanka, known for its golden beaches, lush greenery, and historical landmarks.

History & Heritage

Kalutara has a rich history influenced by ancient kingdoms and colonial rule. The district was an important trading hub during the Portuguese, Dutch, and British periods.

Religious & Cultural Significance

Kalutara is home to the famous Kalutara Bodhiya, a sacred Buddhist temple located along the banks of the Kalu Ganga. The region also boasts diverse cultural influences, with a mix of Buddhist, Hindu, and Christian heritage.

Tourist Attractions

  • Kalutara Bodhiya: A revered Buddhist temple attracting thousands of devotees.
  • Richmond Castle: A grand colonial mansion showcasing British architecture.
  • Kalutara Beach: A stunning coastline perfect for relaxation and water activities.
  • Fa Hien Cave: One of the oldest prehistoric human settlement sites in Sri Lanka.
  • Brief Garden: A beautifully landscaped garden designed by Bevis Bawa.

Culture & Lifestyle

Kalutara is famous for its basket weaving industry, producing intricate handicrafts. The region is also known for its traditional Sri Lankan sweets and seafood cuisine.

Connectivity

Kalutara is easily accessible from Colombo via the Southern Expressway and coastal railway. Public transport and private taxis make traveling convenient for visitors.

With its serene beaches, cultural richness, and historical charm, Kalutara offers a perfect blend of relaxation and exploration for travelers.
