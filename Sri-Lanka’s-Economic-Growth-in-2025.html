Which sectors are driving growth in 2025?

Services and industry have been the primary contributors to the recent rebound. Key drivers include:

  • Tourism & travel-related services: recovery in arrivals supports hotels, transport and retail.
  • Industry (textiles, construction, food manufacturing): construction-led activity and stronger apparel exports have helped industrial output.
  • Services (IT & BPO, finance): IT exports and business process outsourcing continue to expand and support foreign exchange earnings.

Risks, constraints & policy priorities

The main constraints that can alter the 2025 outcome are:

  • External risks: global demand shocks, trade policy changes and volatility in remittances or commodity prices.
  • Fiscal & debt pressures: external debt repayments and the need to maintain IMF program conditions affect fiscal space.
  • Inflation & monetary policy: tighter monetary settings to control inflation can slow near-term growth unless offset by investment.

Policy focus should remain on completing agreed structural reforms, rebuilding reserves, and catalysing private investment to sustain growth beyond 2025.

FAQ ‚Äî quick answers

What is Sri Lanka's GDP growth rate in 2025?

Forecasts differ: the World Bank projects ~3.5% for 2025 while domestic releases and central bank commentary point to a stronger outcome (4%+), reflecting robust Q1 performance. See Sources below for linked reports.

How reliable are the Q1 measurements?

Q1 is official and useful for tracking momentum, but annual figures depend on later quarters and any revisions; the Department of Census & Statistics (DCS) publishes revisions and metadata explaining methodology.
