Kurunegala District

Kurunegala is a historically significant district in Sri Lanka, known for its ancient ruins, religious sites, and bustling economy.

History & Heritage

Kurunegala was once a royal capital of Sri Lanka and is home to many historical sites that highlight the country's rich past.

Natural Beauty

The district is famous for its scenic landscapes, lakes, and the iconic Elephant Rock, which offers panoramic views of the city.

Tourist Attractions

  • Ethugal Kanda (Elephant Rock): A famous landmark providing stunning city views.
  • Ridi Viharaya: An ancient Buddhist temple with intricate carvings and rich history.
  • Kurunegala Lake: A peaceful retreat for nature lovers and joggers.
  • Yapahuwa Rock Fortress: A medieval rock fortress with impressive stone staircases.
  • Padeniya Raja Maha Viharaya: A historic Buddhist temple showcasing unique architecture.

Culture & Lifestyle

Kurunegala thrives with a blend of ancient traditions and modern development, making it a vibrant cultural and economic hub.

Connectivity

The district is well connected by road and rail, ensuring easy access to Colombo, Kandy, and Anuradhapura.

With its deep history, natural beauty, and growing urban landscape, Kurunegala is a must-visit destination in Sri Lanka.
