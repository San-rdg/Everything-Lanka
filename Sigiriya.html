Sigiriya Rock Fortress

**Sigiriya**, also known as the **Lion Rock**, is an **ancient rock fortress** and **UNESCO World Heritage Site**, offering breathtaking views and a rich historical experience.

History & Significance

Built in the **5th century AD** by **King Kashyapa**, Sigiriya was once a royal **palace and fortress**. It later became a **Buddhist monastery** before being rediscovered in modern times.

Main Attractions

  • The **Lion’s Paws**, remnants of a grand entrance to the summit.
  • Beautifully preserved **Sigiriya frescoes**, depicting celestial maidens.
  • The **Mirror Wall**, an ancient reflective surface with historic inscriptions.
  • The **Summit Ruins**, offering a panoramic view of the surrounding landscapes.

How to Get There

  • Located in **Dambulla, Sri Lanka**.
  • About **175 km from Colombo** (~4-hour drive).
  • Easily accessible via **private vehicle, tour buses, or tuk-tuks**.

Best Time to Visit

The best time to visit Sigiriya is **early morning or late afternoon** to avoid the heat and experience stunning sunrises or sunsets.

Interesting Facts

  • Sigiriya was **once considered the 8th Wonder of the World**.
  • The rock rises **200 meters above the jungle**, offering a majestic view.
  • The **ancient hydraulic system** used for the gardens is an engineering marvel.
  • It is one of Sri Lanka’s most visited **historical and archaeological sites**.

Sigiriya is a **must-visit destination** for history lovers, adventurers, and culture enthusiasts, promising an unforgettable journey into Sri Lanka’s glorious past.
