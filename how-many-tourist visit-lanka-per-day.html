How Many Tourists Visit Sri Lanka Per Day?

In 2025, Sri Lanka welcomes an average of 7,500 to 9,000 tourists per day. This figure varies throughout the year, influenced by seasonal travel trends, public holidays, and global travel conditions.

During peak travel seasons — typically from December to April — daily tourist arrivals can exceed 11,000 per day, while the quieter monsoon months like June and September may see numbers dip below 6,500.

Key Facts & Highlights

  • The top tourist source markets for Sri Lanka in 2025 are expected to be India, Russia, UK, Germany, and China.
  • The most popular months for tourist arrivals are January, March, and December.
  • On Sri Lankan New Year week (mid-April), the country experiences a brief tourist dip as locals travel inland.
  • Beach destinations like Mirissa, Unawatuna, and Pasikuda see up to 3,000+ tourists daily during December holidays alone.

Why These Numbers Matter

Tourist arrival figures directly influence hotel occupancy, travel rates, and activity pricing. Knowing the daily average can help travelers plan quieter, affordable visits and avoid overcrowded days in peak seasons.

For travel businesses and tour operators, these stats help predict demand for accommodations, transport, and tour packages.
