Online booking is available for **reserved seats only**. Here‚Äôs what you should know:

üè¢ Buying at Stations

For spontaneous travel or unreserved tickets, head to your nearest railway station.

  • Colombo Fort Station: Largest and most connected hub in Sri Lanka.
  • Reservation Counters: Usually separate from regular ticket counters. Opens at 5:30 AM on weekdays.
  • Tourist Ticket Counters: Ask staff if you qualify for special allocations on scenic trains.

Unreserved class is cheap but gets crowded. If you're traveling with luggage or kids, reserved seating is highly recommended.

ü™ë Train Classes Explained

  • 1st Class (Reserved): Air-conditioned or observation coach. Quiet and clean.
  • 2nd Class (Reserved/Unreserved): Moderate comfort. Some trains have fans but no A/C.
  • 3rd Class: Cheapest option. Often very crowded but a real local experience.

Some trains also offer Observation Cars at the rear/front, especially on the Kandy‚ÄìElla route. Book these early for the best views!

Need to cancel or modify your train booking?

Can I book Sri Lanka train tickets online?

Where can I buy train tickets in person?

At railway stations across Sri Lanka ‚Äî Colombo Fort, Kandy, Ella, Galle, and more.
