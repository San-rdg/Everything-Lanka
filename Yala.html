Yala National Park

**Yala National Park** is the most famous wildlife sanctuary in **Sri Lanka**, renowned for its **diverse ecosystems, rich biodiversity, and high density of leopards**.

Wildlife & Attractions

Home to **elephants, leopards, sloth bears, crocodiles, and over 200 species of birds**, Yala offers an unforgettable **safari experience**. Its mix of **dry forests, grasslands, and coastal lagoons** makes it a haven for nature lovers.

Safari Experience

  • Safari tours available in **early morning and late afternoon**.
  • Guided tours increase chances of spotting **leopards**.
  • Several designated routes through the park ensure **minimal disturbance** to wildlife.

How to Get There

  • Located in the **southeastern region** of Sri Lanka.
  • About **300 km from Colombo** (~6-hour drive).
  • Nearest town: **Tissamaharama**, where most visitors stay.

Best Time to Visit

The best time to visit Yala is **from February to July**, when **water levels are low**, making it easier to spot wildlife.

Interesting Facts

  • Yala has the **highest leopard density** in the world.
  • It covers **979 square kilometers** but only **Block 1** is open for safaris.
  • Home to ancient **Buddhist ruins**, including Sithulpawwa monastery.
  • It has a stunning **coastal stretch** along the Indian Ocean.

Visiting **Yala National Park** is a thrilling experience for **wildlife enthusiasts, photographers, and adventurers**. The beauty of Sri Lanka’s natural world awaits!
