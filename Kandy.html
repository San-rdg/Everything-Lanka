Welcome to Kandy

Kandy District

Kandy is the cultural capital of Sri Lanka, known for its stunning hill-country landscapes, rich history, and spiritual significance.

History & Heritage

Kandy was the last kingdom of Sri Lanka, resisting colonial rule for centuries. It is now a UNESCO World Heritage Site due to its historical importance and cultural richness.

Religious & Cultural Significance

The city is home to the Temple of the Sacred Tooth Relic (Sri Dalada Maligawa), one of the most important Buddhist sites in the world, where a sacred relic of the Buddha is enshrined.

Tourist Attractions

  • Temple of the Tooth: The holiest Buddhist temple in Sri Lanka, attracting thousands of pilgrims.
  • Kandy Lake: A picturesque lake at the heart of the city, perfect for peaceful strolls.
  • Peradeniya Botanical Gardens: A vast collection of exotic plants and flowers.
  • Bahirawakanda Vihara: A giant Buddha statue with panoramic views of Kandy.
  • Kandy Esala Perahera: One of Asia's most spectacular cultural festivals.

Culture & Lifestyle

Kandy maintains a blend of traditional and modern lifestyles. The city is known for its arts, crafts, and Kandyan dance performances.

Connectivity

Kandy is well-connected by road and rail, with scenic train rides from Colombo and Ella being popular among travelers.

With its breathtaking landscapes, deep-rooted traditions, and spiritual ambiance, Kandy offers a unique experience to visitors.
