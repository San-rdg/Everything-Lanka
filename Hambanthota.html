Welcome to Hambantota

Hambantota District

Hambantota is a vibrant district in southern Sri Lanka, known for its coastal beauty, wildlife, and growing infrastructure.

History & Development

Hambantota has historically been a key trading port due to its strategic location along ancient maritime routes. Today, it is rapidly developing with modern infrastructure, including the Hambantota Port and Mattala International Airport.

Wildlife & Nature

Hambantota is home to some of Sri Lanka’s most remarkable wildlife sanctuaries, such as Yala National Park and Bundala National Park, which host diverse species, including leopards, elephants, and flamingos.

Tourist Attractions

  • Yala National Park: Famous for its leopard population and diverse wildlife.
  • Bundala National Park: A paradise for birdwatchers with its rich wetland ecosystem.
  • Hambantota Port: One of Sri Lanka’s key maritime hubs.
  • Mattala International Airport: The second international airport in Sri Lanka.
  • Ridiyagama Safari Park: A unique wildlife experience in the region.

Culture & Lifestyle

Hambantota is a blend of tradition and modernity. Fishing and salt production are integral parts of the economy, while tourism and infrastructure are booming.

Connectivity

With highways, an international airport, and a major port, Hambantota is emerging as a vital economic and transit hub in Sri Lanka.

Hambantota offers a perfect mix of nature, culture, and development, making it an exciting destination for travelers and investors alike.
